Daily Records are the foundation of CicloVital's tracking system. By recording your daily habits and emotional state, you create a valuable dataset that helps you understand patterns, celebrate progress, and identify areas for improvement.

## Overview

Daily Records capture essential wellness metrics:

- **Sleep hours**: How many hours you slept (0-24 hours)
- **Energy level**: Your overall energy throughout the day (0-10 scale)
- **Mood**: Your emotional state and how you felt (0-10 scale)
- **Motivation**: Your drive and motivation level (0-10 scale)
- **Exercise**: Amount of physical activity in hours (0-6 hours)
- **Comments**: Optional notes about your day

<Info>
You can create one record per day. Each record is automatically dated, making it easy to track your wellness journey over time.
</Info>

## Creating a Daily Record

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the Daily Record Modal">
    From the side menu, click the "Registro Diario" (Daily Record) button. This opens the daily record creation form.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Review the Date">
    The form automatically displays today's date. This ensures your record is filed under the correct day.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Enter Sleep Hours">
    In the "Horas de sueño" field, enter how many hours you slept last night. Valid range is 0-24 hours.
    
    <Tip>
    Include naps and all sleep periods to get an accurate total for the day.
    </Tip>
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Rate Your Energy">
    Use the energy slider to rate your energy level from 0 (completely exhausted) to 10 (maximum energy). The slider shows tick marks for each number.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Rate Your Mood">
    Use the "Estado de ánimo" (Mood) slider to rate how you're feeling emotionally from 0 (very low) to 10 (excellent).
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Rate Your Motivation">
    Use the motivation slider to indicate your drive and motivation level from 0 (no motivation) to 10 (highly motivated).
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Enter Exercise Time">
    In the "Ejercicio" field, enter how many hours of physical activity you completed. Valid range is 0-6 hours.
    
    <Note>
    This includes any intentional physical activity: gym workouts, walks, sports, yoga, etc.
    </Note>
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Add Comments (Optional)">
    Use the "Comentario" text area to add any notes about your day. This is optional but helpful for:
    - Recording significant events
    - Explaining unusual scores
    - Tracking what worked well
    - Noting challenges you faced
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Save Your Record">
    Click "Guardar registro" (Save record) to save your daily record. You'll see a confirmation message when it's successfully saved.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Understanding the Rating Scales

All the slider-based metrics use a 0-10 scale:

### Energy Level (0-10)

- **0-3**: Low energy, feeling drained or exhausted
- **4-6**: Moderate energy, able to function but not optimal
- **7-10**: High energy, feeling vibrant and capable

### Mood (0-10)

- **0-3**: Low mood, feeling down or struggling emotionally
- **4-6**: Neutral mood, neither particularly happy nor sad
- **7-10**: Positive mood, feeling good or excellent

### Motivation (0-10)

- **0-3**: Low motivation, difficulty engaging with tasks
- **4-6**: Moderate motivation, some drive present
- **7-10**: High motivation, feeling driven and purposeful

<Tip>
There's no "right" answer for these ratings. Be honest about how you feel each day. Over time, the patterns in your data will be more valuable than any individual score.
</Tip>

## Editing Existing Records

You can update any previous daily record:

1. **Find the record** in your calendar view (see Calendar feature)
2. **Click on the record** to view its details
3. **Click the edit button** (pencil icon)
4. **Update any fields** you want to change
5. **Click "Guardar registro"** to save your changes
6. **See confirmation** that the record was updated successfully

<Info>
Editing is useful if you realize you entered incorrect information or want to add comments after reflecting on your day.
</Info>

## Deleting Records

If you need to remove a daily record:

1. **View the record** in the calendar
2. **Click the delete button** (trash icon)
3. **The record is permanently deleted** from your history

<Note>
Deleted records cannot be recovered. This action is permanent and will affect your statistics and insights.
</Note>

## Validation and Input Rules

The daily record form includes validation to ensure data quality:

### Required Ranges

- **Sleep hours**: Must be between 0 and 24
- **Energy, Mood, Motivation**: Must be between 0 and 10
- **Exercise**: Must be between 0 and 6 hours

### Save Button Behavior

The "Guardar registro" button is:
- **Disabled** when values are outside valid ranges
- **Disabled** while saving is in progress (shows "Guardando...")
- **Enabled** only when all fields contain valid values

<Tip>
If you see a warning message about invalid ranges, check that all your numbers are within the allowed limits.
</Tip>

## Form Features

### Interactive Sliders

The energy, mood, and motivation sliders provide a tactile way to rate your wellness:

- **Tick marks** show each increment from 0-10
- **Snapping**: The slider snaps to whole numbers
- **Current value** displayed next to the slider
- **Easy adjustment** with touch or mouse

### Auto-growing Text Area

The comments field automatically expands as you type:
- No need to scroll within a small box
- Write as much or as little as you want
- Comfortable space for reflection

## Best Practices

<Steps>
  <Step title="Record Consistently">
    Try to fill out your daily record at the same time each day, preferably in the evening when you can reflect on the full day.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Be Honest">
    Record your actual experience, not what you think it "should" be. Honest data leads to valuable insights.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Use Comments Strategically">
    Add comments on days that feel significant—either exceptionally good or challenging. This context helps when reviewing later.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Don't Overthink">
    Your first instinct is usually accurate. Don't spend too much time agonizing over whether you're a 6 or 7.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Review Regularly">
    Use the calendar and statistics features to review your records and spot patterns over time.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Why Daily Records Matter

Consistent daily tracking provides:

### Pattern Recognition

Over time, you'll notice:
- How sleep affects your energy and mood
- Which days you typically feel most motivated
- The impact of exercise on your wellness
- Recurring patterns linked to specific events or days

### Progress Tracking

Daily records help you:
- See improvement over weeks and months
- Celebrate positive trends
- Identify when you need extra support
- Make data-informed decisions about your wellness routine

### AI-Enhanced Insights

Your daily records feed into:
- Statistics dashboard with visualizations
- AI-generated summaries and recommendations
- Personalized insights about your wellness patterns

<Info>
The more consistently you track, the more valuable your data becomes. Even if you miss a few days, just start again—every data point helps build your wellness picture.
</Info>

## Troubleshooting

### Can't Save Record

- Check that all values are within valid ranges
- Ensure you're logged in to your account
- Verify your internet connection

### Record Not Appearing

- Refresh the calendar view
- Check that you received a success confirmation
- Try creating the record again if no confirmation appeared

### Duplicate Records

If you accidentally create multiple records for the same day:
- Keep the most accurate one
- Delete the duplicates using the calendar view
